{{ _('Source: ') }} {{ _('US Norton Blog - Identity theft: What is it, how it works, and common warning signs') }}


{{ _('What is identity theft?') }}

{{ _('Identity theft is a crime where someone illegally obtains and uses another person’s personal information, usually for financial gain.') }}

{{ _('It’s a serious crime that can have devastating effects on victims, including ruined credit, financial loss, and personal distress.') }}

{{ _('How does identity theft happen?') }}

{{ _('Identity thieves use various methods to steal your information, including:') }}

  • {{ _('Phishing: A scam where fraudsters impersonate a legitimate entity to trick you into revealing your personal information.') }}
  • {{ _('Mail theft: Thieves may steal personal mail to get sensitive information, like credit card statements or tax returns.') }}
  • {{ _('Data breaches: Hackers may gain access to online databases that contain personal information.') }}
  • {{ _('Skimming: Fraudsters may use card readers to steal information from your credit or debit cards.') }}

{{ _('Signs of identity theft') }}

{{ _('If you notice any of the following, it could be a sign that your identity has been stolen:') }}

  • {{ _('Unexplained charges on your bank or credit card statements.') }}
  • {{ _('Receiving bills or collection notices for items you didn’t purchase.') }}
  • {{ _('Missing mail or notices that your financial or credit account information has been changed without your consent.') }}

{{ _('How to protect yourself from identity theft') }}

{{ _('To protect yourself from identity theft, you can:') }}

  • {{ _('Use strong, unique passwords for your online accounts.') }}
  • {{ _('Enable two-factor authentication wherever possible.') }}
  • {{ _('Be cautious of unsolicited requests for personal information via phone, email, or text message.') }}
  • {{ _('Monitor your bank accounts and credit reports regularly for any unusual activity.') }}
  • {{ _('Avoid sharing sensitive information on public Wi-Fi networks.') }}

{{ _('What to do if you’re a victim of identity theft?') }}

{{ _('If you become a victim of identity theft, here are the steps to take:') }}

  • {{ _('Report the theft to your bank or credit card company to freeze accounts and protect against further charges.') }}
  • {{ _('Place a fraud alert on your credit report with major credit bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion).') }}
  • {{ _('File a report with the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) and local law enforcement.') }}
  • {{ _('Monitor your credit reports to ensure the thief has not opened new accounts in your name.') }}
